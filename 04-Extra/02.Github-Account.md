

# **GitHub Setup Guide for Interns**

### **How to Follow the Organization & Add It to Your Profile**

Welcome to the team. Follow the steps below to set up your GitHub account properly, join the organization, and complete your profile.

---

## **1. Follow the Organization**

First, make sure you follow the company’s GitHub organization.

**Organization Link:**
[https://github.com/The-One-Branding](https://github.com/The-One-Branding)

### Steps:

1. Open the link above.
2. Sign in to your GitHub account (if you aren’t already).
3. On the organization page, click **Follow** (top-right area).

Following the organization helps you stay updated with public repositories and activity.

---


## **2. Edit and Complete Your GitHub Profile**

A complete profile looks professional and helps you in your career.

### Steps:

1. Click your profile picture (top-right).
2. Click **Your profile**.
3. Click **Edit profile** (button on the right).

### Update the following:

#### **Profile Photo**

* Upload a professional-looking image.

#### **Name**

* Add your full name.

#### **Bio**

Add a short and clear description, for example:
“Frontend Developer | Intern at The One Branding | Learning HTML, CSS, JS”

#### **Location**

* Add your city and country.

#### **Links**

Add:

* LinkedIn profile
* Portfolio website (if any)
* Twitter/X (optional)

#### **README Profile (Optional but Recommended)**

1. Create a new repository named **your-username/your-username**.
2. GitHub will give you the option to create a profile README.
3. Add:

   * About you
   * Skills
   * Projects
   * Contact info

#### **Pinned Repositories**

* Scroll down and click **Customize your pins**.
* Pin your best projects or training tasks.

---

## **3. Keep Your Profile Updated**

As you learn new technologies or complete new projects during the internship, update:

* Skills
* Bio
* Pinned projects
* Links

A good GitHub profile helps you stand out professionally.
